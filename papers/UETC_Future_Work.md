# Future Work and Deferred Topics

This document tracks proposed investigations, analyses, and extensions related to
Unified Electromagnetic Toroidal Cosmology (UETC) that are **explicitly deferred**
from the current canonical framework.

Items listed here do **not** constitute claims, predictions, or unresolved tensions.
They represent areas where further analysis, data review, or formal development
may be warranted in the future.

The presence of an item in this document does not imply necessity, correctness,
or eventual inclusion in the core framework.

---

## Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this document is to:
- preserve ideas and investigative directions without expanding current scope,
- prevent loss of context across development phases,
- maintain discipline between established structure and exploratory work.

Content in this file is intentionally:
- non-binding,
- non-exhaustive,
- and subject to removal or revision.

## Observational and Data-Driven Investigations

Potential future investigations based on existing or emerging observational data:

- Comparative analysis of galactic-scale electromagnetic field morphology across
  different galaxy classes.
- Systematic study of heliospheric boundary asymmetries and time variability as
  constraints on large-scale electromagnetic structure.
- Evaluation of dispersion and propagation constraints inferred from fast radio
  burst (FRB) catalogs in low-density environments.
- Cross-comparison of quasar absorption spectra with intergalactic medium density
  and magnetic-field limits.
- Assessment of whether observed propagation effects admit alternative structural
  interpretations without contradicting established cosmological distance measures.

  are not energetically favored.
- Quantitative comparison between UETC-style scaling descriptions and standard
  gravitational or fluid-dynamical scaling approaches.

## Mathematical and Formal Development

Potential areas for deeper formalization, if warranted:

- Explicit derivation of effective permittivity and permeability functions as
  functions of the propagation parameter \(u(x)\).
- Development of simplified analytic models linking density gradients, field
  curvature, and propagation behavior.
- Exploration of energy partitioning terms within the master energy functional,
  including anisotropic and wave contributions, under constrained assumptions.

These items are deferred pending demonstrated need and clear benefit to structural
clarity.

  relevant measurements already exist.

No new experiments are proposed at this stage.

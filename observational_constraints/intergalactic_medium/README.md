This directory is reserved for observation-driven constraint analyses related to
the intergalactic medium (IGM) and low-density regions between bound astrophysical
systems.

Within UETC, the IGM is treated as a sparsely measured but physically relevant
environment where propagation effects, large-scale electromagnetic structure,
and density gradients may place constraints on cross-scale interpretations.

Potential topics tracked in this section include:
- observational limits on intergalactic magnetic fields,
- dispersion and propagation constraints inferred from fast radio bursts (FRBs),
- absorption and scattering limits derived from quasar spectra,
- large-scale homogeneity and isotropy constraints applicable to low-density media,
- consistency requirements with established cosmological distance measures.

nor does it assert that intergalactic observations require reinterpretation.
